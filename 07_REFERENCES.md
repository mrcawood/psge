## Annotated bibliography (internal summary)

Annotated Bibliography – Key Variegate Porphyria Structural Studies

Meissner, P.N., Dailey, T.A., Hift, R.J., Ziman, M., Corrigall, A.V., Roberts, A.G., et al. (1996)
A R59W mutation in human protoporphyrinogen oxidase results in decreased enzyme activity and is prevalent in South Africans with variegate porphyria. Nature Genetics, 13(1):95–97.
This seminal paper identifies the R59W substitution in PPOX as a founder mutation in South African variegate porphyria (VP). Functional assays showed that the R59W variant leads to reduced PPO enzyme activity, supporting a biochemical basis for disease association. The mutation was found in virtually all VP patients studied and absent in controls, establishing a clear genotype–biochemical phenotype link.
Relevance: Direct mechanistic evidence for your case study; confirms that structural variation at R59 impacts enzyme function.  ￼

⸻

Qin, X., Tan, Y., Wang, L., Wang, Z., Wang, B., Wen, X., Yang, G., Xi, Z. & Shen, Y. (2011)
Structural insight into human variegate porphyria disease. FASEB Journal.
This study presents a 1.9 Å crystal structure of human PPO (hPPO) complexed with its cofactor (FAD) and an inhibitor. The analysis provides detailed molecular interactions, showing how the R59W mutation likely causes electrostatic hindrance in the FAD binding pocket due to the bulky tryptophan side chain. The study also kinetically characterized numerous VP-causing mutations in vitro.
Relevance: Offers high-resolution human structural templates for variant modeling and contextualizes variant impacts at the molecular level.  ￼

⸻

Wang, B., Wen, X., Qin, X., Wang, Z., Tan, Y., Shen, Y., & Xi, Z. (2013)
Quantitative structural insight into human variegate porphyria disease. Journal of Biological Chemistry (via PMC).
This work uses a combination of molecular dynamics (MD) simulations and statistical analysis to quantitatively relate the dynamic behavior of hPPO mutants to catalytic activity. The authors found that the probability of sampling “privileged conformations” during MD correlates strongly with measured enzyme kinetics (R² > 0.9), suggesting that dynamic sampling patterns can predict catalytic efficiency of clinically observed mutations.
Relevance: A structural + dynamics approach that closely mirrors what your pipeline might aim to formalize — linking conformational behavior to functional impact.  ￼

⸻

Contextual Note on Variegate Porphyria

Variegate porphyria (VP) is a low-penetrance, autosomal dominant metabolic disorder caused by mutations in the PPOX gene, leading to partial deficiency of protoporphyrinogen oxidase and resulting in disrupted heme synthesis. Clinical manifestations vary widely, including photosensitivity and acute neurovisceral attacks. Mutations may show heterogeneous biochemical and clinical effects, underscoring the complexity of genotype–phenotype relationships in VP.  ￼
